AishiForFurries is a registered NGO
dedicated to rescuing, rehabilitating,
feeding, and caring for stray animals
across Gurgaon ensuring they receive
the dignity, health, and love every
living being deserves.

Objective & Social
Impact

Our aim is to build a compassionate environment where every stray
animal receives necessary medical care, shelter, and love. Through
continuous awareness and community engagement, we foster a
culture of compassion, enabling us to create lasting change and
promote responsible pet ownership across Gurgaon.

Case Stories: Lakshmi, Narayan and Khushi

Lakshmi was thrown into a 40-ft dry well. Narayan was found after a hit-and-run. Khushi was discovered dragging himself across an empty plot. All three had severe
spinal fractures. Every doctor said nothing could be done.
But giving up was never an option.
They now live under our care — safe, supported, and loved. They each have a custom wheelchair that they use to move freely, enjoy the sun, and experience joy in
their own way.
Their bodies may not heal, but their spirits have, with comfort, dignity, and a life worth living.
Case Stories: Blacky

Blacky was one of the first dogs we cared for. After a major leg fracture, his first amputation gave him a second chance at life. But with age and weight-related strain,
his other front leg also got fractured and had to be amputated again.
Despite all medical efforts and his incredible will to fight, Blacky couldn’t recover from the second surgery.
His journey reminds us why consistent community care and responsible feeding are essential for our indies.
Case Stories: Rani

A reporter called about Rani, found unable to move after a hit-and-run. Her spine was fractured, and the chances of recovery were almost zero. But we tried, after
surgery and weeks of care, Rani did the impossible, she walks and runs again.
Because her spine is delicate, she now lives safely at our shelter for the rest of her life, happy and pain-free.
A life saved because we didn’t give up.
Case Stories

We received a call about a cat found with severe burn injuries. By the time we reached her, one leg was beyond saving and had to be amputated, and the rest
of his tiny body carried deep, painful burns.Doctors warned us that her chances of survival were slim but we chose hope.

For five days, he fought with everything he had while we made sure he was never alone, never scared, and never in pain.
He eventually passed away, but not on the streets... he left this world held, cared for, and loved.
